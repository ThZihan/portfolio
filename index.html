American Astronautical Society's CanSat Missions

Participated in projects simulating planetary exploration missions:

  • 2023 Mission: Focused on the Planetary Probe Landing Mission, simulating the descent and safe landing of a probe designed to collect surface data in extreme environments. Tasks included developing deployment mechanisms and ensuring sensor data integrity upon landing.
  • 2024 Mission: The Planetary Probe Instrument Delivery Mission involved designing and simulating a system capable of delivering scientific instruments to a target planetary zone with high precision. This year's mission emphasized payload delivery, resource optimization, and real-time telemetry during the descent phase.

These missions were mimicked using CanSat platforms, providing valuable experience in space mission simulations and engineering challenges associated with extraterrestrial environments.

Recirculating Aquaculture Monitoring System

As the System Engineer, contributed to the development of a real-time monitoring system for recirculating aquaculture setups. This project, a collaboration between IUB Fablab and the SAU Aquaculture Department, aimed to enhance the sustainability of aquaculture systems through:

  • Continuous monitoring of water quality parameters like oxygen levels, temperature, pH, Salinity etc.
  • Integrating IoT sensors and cloud analytics to enable predictive maintenance and ensure optimal conditions for aquatic organisms.
  • Providing a user-friendly dashboard for aquaculture professionals to make data-driven decisions.

This innovative approach promotes sustainable practices and boosts efficiency in aquaculture industries.
